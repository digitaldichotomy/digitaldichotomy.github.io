## Step 1: Establish the Legal Foundation -- Define the Tort {#step-1-establish-the-legal-foundation-define-the-tort .text-2xl .font-bold .dark:text-white .mb-4}

::: {.bg-gray-900/50 .p-4 .rounded .border-l-4 .border-gray-700}
### The Core Claim: Digital Battery (Non-Contact Physiological Trespass) {#the-core-claim-digital-battery-non-contact-physiological-trespass .text-xl .font-bold .text-green-400 .mb-2}

Under common law, battery is: \"The intentional, unpermitted, and
harmful or offensive touching of another person.\"

Modern biometric harvesting meets all three criteria:

::: overflow-x-auto
  Element              How It\'s Met
  -------------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Intentional          Companies design systems to extract bio-signals (rPPG, voice analysis, HRV). Patents prove intent.
  Unpermitted          No user consents to having their heartbeat read by a camera or their arousal inferred from touch pressure.
  Offensive Touching   Remote reading of heart rate, brain state, or emotional response is a violation of bodily integrity---even without physical contact.
:::
:::
:::

## Legal Framework: Biometric Battery as Novel Tort {#legal-framework-biometric-battery-as-novel-tort .text-2xl .font-bold .dark:text-white .mb-4}

::: {.bg-gray-900/50 .p-4 .rounded .border-l-4 .border-gray-700}
### The Core Theory: Digital Battery (Non-Contact Physiological Trespass) {#the-core-theory-digital-battery-non-contact-physiological-trespass .text-xl .font-bold .text-green-400 .mb-2}

Biometric battery reframes unauthorized physiological data collection
not as privacy intrusion, but as bodily trespass.

Common law battery requires three elements: (1) defendant act, (2)
offensive contact, (3) no consent.

Contemporary harm: Treats the body as a sovereign entity whose
boundaries may not be crossed without permission.

::: {.bg-blue-900/30 .border-l-4 .border-blue-500 .p-4 .mt-4}
#### Precedent Support {#precedent-support .text-lg .font-bold .text-blue-300 .mb-2}

-   **Illinois Biometric Information and Privacy Act (BIPA)** --
    Established that statutory violations alone confer standing even in
    absence of demonstrable financial harm.
-   *Jane Doe v. Meta (2028, CA)* -- First recognition rPPG violation as
    privacy invasion.
-   *ICBE v. NeuroSynaptic Global (2032)* -- First ruling classifying
    neural inference as bio-physiological trespass.
