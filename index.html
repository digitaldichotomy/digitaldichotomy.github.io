About

This research explores the intricate mechanisms by which digital systems create pathways for self-surveillance and identity manipulation. Through the lens of computational architecture, we examine how modern technology facilitates covert intrusion into personal cognitive spaces.

Our work investigates the intersection of cybersecurity, digital psychology, and architectural theory to understand how virtual environments shape human behavior and self-perception in unprecedented ways.

The project combines theoretical frameworks from multiple disciplines to create a comprehensive understanding of digital identity formation and the hidden vulnerabilities within our technological infrastructure.
