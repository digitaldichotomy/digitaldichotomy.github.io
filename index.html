Acoustic Eavesdropping: The Laser Microphone

Sound waves inside a room cause any windowpane to vibrate minutely. By reflecting a laser beam off the glass and analyzing the microscopic distortions in the reflection, an operator can perfectly reconstruct conversations from hundreds of meters away. The window itself becomes the microphone diaphragm.

RF Sensing: The Light Bulb Bug

An incandescent or LED light bulb is a sensitive piece of electronics. Speech vibrations in a room cause the bulb's filament or housing to move, which in turn modulates its electromagnetic emissions. An RF receiver can capture and demodulate these signals to listen to conversations. The light fixture becomes a covert listening device.

Behavioral Analysis: Smart Meter Spying (NILM)

Non-Intrusive Load Monitoring (NILM) analyzes the electrical noise on your home's power lines. By analyzing historical patterns, an outside system can determine when specific appliances are turned on or off—when you make coffee, watch TV, or use a computer—building a detailed profile of your daily routines without a single bug inside your home.
