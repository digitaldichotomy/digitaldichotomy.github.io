Excerpts from the Surveillance Technologies Compendium. The mundane is the primary weapon of the panopticon.

Acoustic: Laser Microphone

Sound waves inside a room cause any windowpane to vibrate minutely. By reflecting a laser beam off the glass and analyzing the microscopic phase shifts in the reflection (optical interferometry), an operator can reconstruct conversations from a secure standoff distance. The window itself becomes the microphone.

Electromagnetic: RF Light Bulb Analysis

A common light bulb is a sensitive electronic component. Speech vibrations in a room cause the bulb's filament or housing to move, minutely modulating its electromagnetic emissions. A remote RF receiver can capture and demodulate these signals to listen in. The light fixture becomes an unwitting bug.

Behavioral: Smart Meter Spying (NILM)

Non-Intrusive Load Monitoring (NILM) analyzes the unique electrical noise signatures on a building's power lines. By analyzing these patterns, an outside system can determine when specific appliances are used—when you make coffee, watch TV, or use a computer—building a detailed profile of your daily routines without a single device inside your home.
