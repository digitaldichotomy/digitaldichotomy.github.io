The Sanity of 'Paranoia'

November 03, 2025

Let’s get one thing straight. You didn’t agree to any of this. Not really. You clicked "I Agree" on a 70-page document you didn't read to check your bank balance. You let an app track your location so you could order a pizza. You needed a phone for two-factor authentication just to log into your own damn email. These weren't choices. They were tolls. The price of admission to a society that has already been built, wired, and sold while we were busy living our lives. The biggest lie they ever sold us is the idea of "opting out." It’s a joke. It’s a pressure-release valve designed to make us feel like we have some agency. Go ahead, delete your social media. See how long you can last without a messaging app for work, without an email for a job application, without a digital footprint to prove you even exist. Trying to "opt out" is like trying to opt out of using money. You can do it, technically, but you’ll be living in the woods. They've made participation mandatory. This isn't a service we subscribe to; it's the air we breathe. And the most brilliant, most insidious part of the whole machine? The gaslighting. Bring this up in conversation and watch what happens. You're not concerned; you're "paranoid." You're not observant; you're "a conspiracy theorist." You're not seeing the architecture of control; you're "spending too much time online." They built a prison, made it comfortable, and then convinced half the inmates to guard the other half by calling anyone who points at the bars crazy. It is a masterpiece of psychological control. So no, nobody is "willingly" doing shit. We're complying. We're trying to get by in a system that was put in place long before we understood the terms. The first step isn't "opting out." The first step is admitting to yourself that you've been opted-in since birth. That the contract was signed in your sleep. That you're not paranoid for seeing the bars on the window. You're just finally awake.
