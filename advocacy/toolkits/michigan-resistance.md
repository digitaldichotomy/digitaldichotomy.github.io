### Michigan Eavesdropping Act (MCL 750.539) {#michigan-eavesdropping-act-mcl-750.539 .text-2xl .font-bold .text-blue-300 .mb-4}

::: text-gray-300
Michigan\'s eavesdropping statutes provide some of the strongest
protections against unauthorized biometric data collection:

::: {.bg-blue-900/30 .border .border-blue-700 .rounded-lg .p-4 .mb-4}
#### Key Provisions: {#key-provisions .font-bold .text-blue-200 .mb-2}

-   ‚Ä¢ Prohibits unauthorized \"electronic surveillance\" of private
    communications
-   ‚Ä¢ Defines \"eavesdropping\" to include unauthorized recording or
    monitoring
-   ‚Ä¢ Allows for civil penalties of up to \$5,000 per violation plus
    attorney fees
-   ‚Ä¢ Provides for actual damages and punitive damages for willful
    violations
:::

Biometric harvesting via rPPG and sensor fusion systems can be
classified as unauthorized electronic surveillance under these statutes
because:

1.  Cameras and sensors \"record\" physiological responses without
    knowledge or consent
2.  Facial micro-expressions and heart rate patterns constitute
    \"private communications\"
3.  The systems are designed to conceal their surveillance nature from
    users
4.  Data is transmitted to third parties without user permission
:::
:::

::: {.bg-gray-900/60 .border .border-gray-700 .rounded-lg .p-6}
### Common Law Battery Claims {#common-law-battery-claims .text-2xl .font-bold .text-green-300 .mb-4}

::: text-gray-300
Michigan courts recognize battery claims for non-contact physiological
violations:

::: {.bg-green-900/30 .border .border-green-700 .rounded-lg .p-4}
#### Battery Elements in Michigan: {#battery-elements-in-michigan .font-bold .text-green-200 .mb-2}

1.  \*\*Intentional Act\*\*: Companies design systems to extract
    bio-signals
2.  \*\*Harmful/Offensive Contact\*\*: Remote reading of heart rate,
    brain state, emotional response
3.  \*\*Without Consent\*\*: No user consents to having bio-fields read
    by cameras/sensors
4.  \*\*Damages\*\*: Medical expenses, therapy costs, emotional
    distress, lost wages
:::

Michigan precedent expands battery to include technological
\"touching\" - where systems electronically interact with a person\'s
biological processes without permission.
:::
:::

::: {.bg-gray-900/60 .border .border-gray-700 .rounded-lg .p-6}
### Michigan Consumer Protection Act {#michigan-consumer-protection-act .text-2xl .font-bold .text-yellow-300 .mb-4}

::: text-gray-300
The MPCA provides additional legal avenues for biometric privacy
violations:

::: {.bg-yellow-900/30 .border .border-yellow-700 .rounded-lg .p-4}
#### MPCA Provisions: {#mpca-provisions .font-bold .text-yellow-200 .mb-2}

-   ‚Ä¢ Prohibits \"unfair, unconscionable, or deceptive methods, acts, or
    practices\"
-   ‚Ä¢ Allows for actual damages plus up to \$250,000 in punitive damages
-   ‚Ä¢ Allows for treble damages for willful violations
-   ‚Ä¢ Provides for attorney fees and costs
:::

Failure to disclose biometric harvesting practices constitutes deceptive
trade practices under the MPCA.
:::
