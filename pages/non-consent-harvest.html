Biometric Battery Doctrine

The legal framework for challenging non-consent biometric harvesting rests on the biometric battery doctrine: unauthorized biological data collection constitutes battery‚Äîthe unlawful, non-consensual touching of one's biological processes by another. This doctrine provides clear legal standing for victims and establishes significant liability for violators.

Legal Elements Required:

  • Unauthorized Contact: Collection of biological data without informed consent
  • Biological Harm: Measurable interference with biological processes
  • Trespass to Chattels: Unauthorized intrusion upon biological property
  • Dissemination: Distribution or sale of biological data without permission
  • Damages: Quantifiable harm from privacy invasion and economic loss

Remedies and Damages

Compensatory Damages: $1,000-$2,500 per biometric violation (statutory minimum)

Punitive Damages: Up to 3x compensatory damages for willful violations

Injunctive Relief: Court orders to cease all biometric data collection

Restitution: Return of all profits derived from biological data

Destruction Orders: Mandatory destruction of all biological data collected
