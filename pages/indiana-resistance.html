Indiana Biometric Privacy Act (IBPA)

Indiana's IBPA provides strong protections for biometric data privacy:

Key Provisions:

  • ‚Ä¢ Requires informed consent before collecting biometric data
  • ‚Ä¢ Prohibits sale of biometric information without consent
  • ‚Ä¢ Allows for damages of $1,000 per negligent violation
  • ‚Ä¢ Provides for $5,000 per reckless/intentional violation
  • ‚Ä¢ Provides for attorney fees and costs

Apple's sensor fusion systems violate the IBPA by:

  1. Collecting motion, touch, and usage data to infer emotional/cognitive states
  2. Processing this data to create biometric identifiers without consent
  3. Failed to disclose the nature and purpose of biometric data collection
  4. Transmitting inferred biometric data to third-party servers

Common Law Invasion of Privacy

Indiana recognizes invasion of privacy claims for technological violations:

Privacy Torts in Indiana:

  • **Intrusion upon solitude**: Unauthorized collection of private biometric data
  • **Public disclosure**: Revealing private biometric characteristics
  • **False light**: Misrepresenting the nature of data collection
  • **Appropriation**: Using biometric characteristics for commercial purposes

Indiana courts have extended privacy protections to cover emerging technologies that intrude on personal autonomy.
