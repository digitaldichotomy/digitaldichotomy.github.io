The Architecture of Invasion operates as a continuous closed-loop system, broken into three distinct phases. It begins by passively reading biological signals, models that data into a predictive twin, and ends by actively influencing the target to achieve a desired outcome.

Phase 1: The "Sensor" Layer (Tiers I-III)

The "Sensor" layer captures biological signals without physical contact by weaponizing common devices. A standard camera becomes a biometric sensor (rPPG), and ambient Wi-Fi signals become a through-wall motion detector (CSI Analysis). These vectors are passive, covert, and already deployed.

Key Sensor Vectors

  • Tier I: rPPG & Gaze Tracking

    Uses cameras to read heart rate variability (stress) and pupil dilation (cognitive load). Proven in Meta & Intel patents.

  • Tier II: Device-Based Inference

    Uses a phone's motion sensors (accelerometer) to infer keystrokes and passwords. Proven in academic research.

  • Tier III: Wi-Fi CSI Analysis

    Uses standard Wi-Fi router signals to detect presence, motion, and even breathing patterns through solid walls. Proven by MIT research and now a commercial product.

Proven Accuracy of Sensor Vectors

Research has validated the high accuracy of these covert collection methods, proving their viability as reliable data sources for the Digital Twin.

Phase 2: The "Model" Layer (Tiers IV-V)

The "Model" layer fuses the raw sensor data into a coherent, predictive simulation. It adds biochemical context (Tier IV) by analyzing exhaled breath (VOCs) and societal context (Tier V) by using infrastructural sensors like IMSI-catchers and "Smart City" microphones.

Building the Digital Twin

The Digital Twin is not just a user profile; it is a high-resolution model of a person's biological and cognitive state. By combining sensor data with context, the system can move from observation to prediction.


For example, Tier I rPPG may show high stress. Tier IV VOC analysis can confirm if this is metabolic (from illness) or hormonal (from anxiety). Tier V location data shows the context (at work vs. at a protest). This fusion creates a powerful predictive asset.

Composition of a Digital Twin (Projected)

The Digital Twin's composition is evolving from purely behavioral data to a model dominated by involuntary biological and infrastructural inputs.

Phase 3: The "Actuator" Layer (Tiers VI-VIII)

This is the endgame: the pivot from passively "reading" you to actively "writing" to you. The "Actuator" layer uses the Digital Twin's predictions to modulate your environment, neurology, and economic access to guarantee a specific outcome.

Key Actuator Vectors

  • Tier VI: Directed-Energy Systems

    Mobile platforms (drones) and fixed systems that use pulsed RF energy (microwaves) to cause neurological effects. Validated by NIH/NASEM analysis of "Havana Syndrome."

  • Tier VII: 'Write to Brain' Interface

    Non-invasive, bidirectional brain interfaces. DARPA's N3 program explicitly seeks a device that can "both 'read' from and 'write' to" the brain.

  • Tier VIII: Economic & Info Control

    Using programmable CBDCs to restrict transactions based on your bio-profile, or algorithmically targeting content to exploit your *known* emotional state (proven in Facebook's "Emotional Contagion" study).

Analysis of Control Vectors

The Actuator layer provides a multi-pronged approach to behavioral modulation, from overt economic restrictions to covert neurological influence.
