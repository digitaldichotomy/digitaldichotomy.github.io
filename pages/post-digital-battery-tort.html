LEGAL FRAMEWORK

The New Tort of Digital Battery

Redefining digital surveillance from privacy violation to physical battery—unauthorized biological contact with measurable harm.

November 27, 2025 12 min read

Beyond Privacy: The Need for Legal Evolution

The current legal framework of "privacy" is not merely inadequate; it is fundamentally obsolete. It operates on a false premise, treating the pervasive violation of the digital age as a simple matter of information theft—the unauthorized extraction of data points. This perspective fails to grasp the true nature of the offense.

The Legal Innovation

We propose the tort of Digital Battery, defined using the rigorous, time-tested standard of common tort law: the intentional, harmful, or offensive contact with another person without their consent. The critical innovation is the redefinition of the boundaries of the 'person' and the meaning of 'contact' in the age of omnipresent sensing.

The Four Elements of Digital Battery

To establish the compelling necessity and legal viability of this new tort, we must prove the existence of all four essential elements: Intent, Contact, Harm, and Lack of Consent.

1 Intent: Design as Malice

In the context of Digital Battery, Intent is demonstrated not by a subjective analysis of malice or ill will on the part of the system's operator, but by the patented, public design and documented functionality of the systems themselves. The architecture and technical specifications are the admission of guilt.

The System's Own Documentation

The intent to contact, measure, and influence the biological state is embedded in the system's blueprint. Patents, technical specifications, and marketing materials serve as prima facie evidence of deliberate design for biological intrusion.

2 Contact: The Physics of Remote Touch

Contact is legally made the moment a sensor field, algorithm, or modulated signal intersects with the human biological process to measure, map, or modulate it. This includes, but is not limited to, technologies like remote photoplethysmography (rPPG) reading a pulse from meters away, radiofrequency (RF) signals monitoring respiration patterns, or infrasonic tones used for location mapping that resonate with the inner ear.

The Invisible Touch

The distinction between a physical 'signal' and a physical 'touch' has collapsed under the weight of modern biosensing technology. The act of remote reading is the act of physical contact. The invisible electromagnetic and acoustic fields generated by these systems are the new fingers of corporate intrusion.

3 Harm: Disruption of Homeostasis

Harm is fundamentally defined as the disruption of neurochemical homeostasis. This harm is quantifiable through biological metrics: the documentation of induced anxiety spikes, the systematic fragmentation of natural sleep cycles, and the exhaustion and dysregulation of the dopamine reward pathways.

Measurable Biological Damage

The resulting chronic stress, learned helplessness, and cognitive fatigue are not subjective experiences—they are tangible injuries of the Digital Age. These conditions can be medically documented and correlated with specific digital exposures and manipulations.

4 Lack of Consent: The Coercion of the Fine Print

Lack of Consent is the cornerstone upon which the entire tort rests. Consent that is buried in lengthy, opaque End-User License Agreements (EULAs) or achieved through pre-checked boxes is systemic coercion. True consent must be specific, revocable, and based on a full, clear, and real-time understanding of the biological intrusion being authorized.

The Illusion of Choice

The current model of "consent" is a legal fiction designed to protect corporate interests rather than individual rights. The complexity and opacity of modern digital terms create an asymmetry of understanding that vitiates the very concept of informed agreement.

Supporting Legal Precedents

Traditional Battery Law

The tort of battery has long-standing legal precedent requiring only: (1) intentional contact, (2) harmful or offensive contact, and (3) lack of consent. Digital Battery extends this established framework to new technological contexts while maintaining legal consistency.

Privacy vs. Bodily Integrity

Current privacy law treats digital intrusion as information theft. Digital Battery reframes it as what it is: unauthorized physical intervention into the human nervous, endocrine, and circulatory systems.

International Human Rights Law

The Digital Battery tort aligns with established principles of bodily integrity and autonomy found in international human rights conventions, including the right to security of person and freedom from non-consensual experimentation.

Implementation Strategy

Individual Legal Action

The Notice of Digital Battery serves as a formal legal instrument that individuals can serve to corporations, establishing a clear paper trail and creating legal liability for unauthorized biological contact.

Class Action Certification

For widespread impact, class action lawsuits can establish precedent and create systemic change, forcing corporations to modify their invasive practices and establish new industry standards.

Legislative Advocacy

The Biological Integrity Act would codify the Digital Battery tort into law, creating a private right of action for individuals and establishing clear liability standards for corporate biological intrusion.
