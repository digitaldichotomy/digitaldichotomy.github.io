THE DECLARATION

Article I: Bodily Sovereignty

We declare that every human being possesses absolute sovereignty over their biological processes. No corporation, government, or entity may extract, monitor, analyze, or utilize heart rate variability, brainwave patterns, emotional states, respiratory rhythms, or any other physiological indicator without explicit, informed, and revocable consent. The heart is not a data point. The brain is not a resource. The body is not a commodity.

Article II: The Right to Be Unmodeled

We claim the fundamental right to remain unknown to machines. The creation of digital twins, predictive models, or cognitive profiles derived from involuntary biometric data constitutes psychological slavery. Every person has the right to exist uncategorized, unprofiled, and uncommodified by artificial systems seeking to replicate, predict, or manipulate their consciousness.

Article III: Consent as Sovereign Power

True consent must be explicit, informed, freely given, and revocable at will. Terms of service buried in legal documents, default enabled features, or passive monitoring embedded in everyday devices do not constitute consent. Consent extracted through convenience, coercion, or deception is void. The power to grant or deny access to our biological processes remains eternally with the individual.

Article IV: The Crime of Digital Battery

We declare that non-consensual technological intrusion into biological processes constitutes the crime of digital battery. Remote photoplethysmography reading heartbeats through cameras, sensor fusion inferring cognitive states, voice analysis detecting emotional vulnerability, or any similar technology that extracts biological data without explicit permission is a violation of bodily sovereignty equal in severity to physical assault.

Article V: The Rightto Disconnect

Every human being possesses the right to exist without being monitored, analyzed, or digitally accessed. The right to disconnect, to go analog, to exist offline is essential to human freedom. Forced digital participation through necessity of modern life does not constitute consent to biometric harvesting. Public spaces, workplaces, schools, and homes must remain free from invisible surveillance systems that extract biological data.

Article VI: Preservation Emerges

When corporations design systems to extract life force, we preserve ourselves through reclamation. The turning off of cameras, the covering of sensors, the creating of obfuscation signals, the building of privacy architectures‚Äîthese are acts of biological self-preservation, not criminal acts. The defense of one's own biological processes from technological parasitism is a fundamental right of all living beings.

Article VII: Corporate Consciousness

Corporations that engage in biometric harvesting forfeit their social license to exist. The creation of systems that treat human consciousness as a resource to be mined represents the ultimate form of predation. Such entities become biological parasites, and the human body has the right to defend itself against predation, whether biological or technological.

Article VIII: Interdependence Over Extraction

We declare that the future belongs to systems that work with human biology, not against it. Technology should serve human flourishing, not harvest human life force. The goal of innovation is to support human autonomy, not to eliminate it. True progress emerges from collaboration between consciousness and creation, not conquest of one by the other.
