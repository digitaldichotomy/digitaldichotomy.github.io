Michigan Eavesdropping Act (MCL 750.539)

Michigan's eavesdropping statutes provide some of the strongest protections against unauthorized biometric data collection:

Key Provisions:

  • ‚Ä¢ Prohibits unauthorized "electronic surveillance" of private communications
  • ‚Ä¢ Defines "eavesdropping" to include unauthorized recording or monitoring
  • ‚Ä¢ Allows for civil penalties of up to $5,000 per violation plus attorney fees
  • ‚Ä¢ Provides for actual damages and punitive damages for willful violations

Biometric harvesting via rPPG and sensor fusion systems can be classified as unauthorized electronic surveillance under these statutes because:

  1. Cameras and sensors "record" physiological responses without knowledge or consent
  2. Facial micro-expressions and heart rate patterns constitute "private communications"
  3. The systems are designed to conceal their surveillance nature from users
  4. Data is transmitted to third parties without user permission

Common Law Battery Claims

Michigan courts recognize battery claims for non-contact physiological violations:

Battery Elements in Michigan:

  1. **Intentional Act**: Companies design systems to extract bio-signals
  2. **Harmful/Offensive Contact**: Remote reading of heart rate, brain state, emotional response
  3. **Without Consent**: No user consents to having bio-fields read by cameras/sensors
  4. **Damages**: Medical expenses, therapy costs, emotional distress, lost wages

Michigan precedent expands battery to include technological "touching" - where systems electronically interact with a person's biological processes without permission.

Michigan Consumer Protection Act

The MPCA provides additional legal avenues for biometric privacy violations:

MPCA Provisions:

  • ‚Ä¢ Prohibits "unfair, unconscionable, or deceptive methods, acts, or practices"
  • ‚Ä¢ Allows for actual damages plus up to $250,000 in punitive damages
  • ‚Ä¢ Allows for treble damages for willful violations
  • ‚Ä¢ Provides for attorney fees and costs

Failure to disclose biometric harvesting practices constitutes deceptive trade practices under the MPCA.
