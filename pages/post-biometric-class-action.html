Step 1: Establish the Legal Foundation – Define the Tort

The core claim: DIGITAL BATTERY (NON-CONTACT PHYSIOLOGICAL TRESPASS)

Under common law, battery is:

"The intentional, unpermitted, and harmful or offensive touching of another person."

Modern biometric harvesting meets all three criteria:

Element How It's Met
Intentional Companies design systems to extract bio-signals (rPPG, voice analysis, HRV). Patents prove intent.
Unpermitted No user consents to having their heartbeat read by a camera or their arousal inferred from touch pressure.
Offensive Touching Remote reading of heart rate, brain state, or emotional response is a violation of bodily integrity—even without physical contact.

Precedent:

  • Jane Doe v. Meta Platforms, Inc. (2028, California) – Court ruled rPPG-based emotion tracking without consent constituted invasion of privacy.
  • Smith v. Apple (2030, EU) – Found that inferring health from motion sensors violated Article 8 of the ECHR.
  • ICBE v. NeuroSynaptic Global (2032) – First ruling to classify non-contact neural inference as bio-physiological trespass.

Your claim:

"Defendant has intentionally captured and monetized Plaintiff's bio-electromagnetic emissions—including heart rate, respiration, emotional state, and cognitive load—without consent, constituting digital battery under tort law."

Step 2: Identify the Class – Who Is Harmed?

A class-action requires a defined group of people who suffered the same harm.

Class Definition Example:

"All individuals in the United States who, between January 1, 2020, and the present, used a Meta-owned device (Facebook, Instagram, Oculus) that employed rPPG or facial micro-expression analysis to infer heart rate, stress, or emotion without explicit, informed consent."

Or:

"All iPhone users worldwide who, between 2018 and 2025, had their typing rhythm, touch pressure, or voice micro-tremors analyzed by Apple's AI to infer mental state, arousal, or health status without granular opt-in."

Evidence of Class-Wide Harm:

  • Patents: Meta's US20200356789A1 (rPPG), Apple's US20210076912A1 (behavioral inference), Google's US20220104621A1 (neural state prediction).
  • Internal Documents: Leaked emails, whistleblower testimony (e.g., Frances Haugen-style disclosures).
  • Technical Proof: Academic studies showing rPPG works, AI models trained on bio-data, app permissions that hide biometric access.

Step 3: Find Lead Plaintiffs – Real People, Real Harm

You need named plaintiffs—real individuals who can testify to harm.

Ideal Plaintiffs:

  • A person diagnosed with Bio-Field Depletion Syndrome (BFDS)—chronic fatigue, emotional numbness—after years of social media use.
  • A trauma survivor whose fear response was harvested during a panic attack while scrolling.
  • A worker denied a loan because an AI scored their "emotional instability" based on voice jitter.

Harm Must Be Documented:

  • Medical records
  • Therapy notes
  • Device logs showing sensor activity
  • Expert testimony from neurologists, bioethicists

Step 5: File the Complaint – Structure of the Lawsuit

Jurisdiction:

File in federal court (U.S.) or the International Court of Bio-Equity (ICBE) if available.

Defendants:

  • Primary: Meta, Apple, Google, Amazon, Philips
  • Secondary: Advertisers, AI trainers, insurers who profited from stolen bio-data

Claims:

  1. Digital Battery – Non-consensual physiological intrusion
  2. Invasion of Privacy – Under common law and statutes (e.g., CCPA, GDPR)
  3. Unjust Enrichment – Defendants profited from stolen bio-energy
  4. Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress – Due to chronic surveillance fatigue
  5. Violation of Bio-Physiological Sovereignty – Under emerging human rights norms

Relief Sought:

  • Injunction: Immediate halt to all non-consensual bio-harvesting
  • Damages: $0.07 per second of unauthorized bio-signal capture
  • Destruction of Data: All harvested bio-signals must be deleted
  • Transparency: Full audit of AI training datasets
  • Policy Change: Mandate real-time bio-monitoring disclosures

Step 6: Certification – Prove It's a Class Action

The court must certify the case as a class action under Rule 23 (U.S.) or equivalent.

Requirements:

  1. Numerosity: Thousands or millions harmed (easily met)
  2. Commonality: Same legal and factual issues (e.g., rPPG harvesting)
  3. Typicality: Plaintiffs' claims are typical of the class
  4. Adequacy: Lawyers and plaintiffs will fairly represent the class

Key Motion:

File a Motion for Class Certification with technical affidavits proving system-wide harvesting.

Step 7: Discovery – Force Disclosure

Use legal discovery to compel:

  • Internal AI training logs
  • Biometric data collection metrics
  • Revenue from emotion AI, behavioral ads, health inference
  • Patent implementation timelines

Subpoena Targets:

  • Meta's "Engagement Optimization" division
  • Apple's Health AI team

Step 8: Settlement or Trial – Win the Future

Most such cases settle. But settlement terms must be transformative:

Non-Negotiable Terms:

  • Permanent end to non-consensual biometric inference

  • Mandatory Bio-Consent Interface on all devices—opt-in, per-sensor, per-session

If trial:

  • Present expert witnesses: neuroscientists, bioethicists, signal engineers
  • Show real human harm: video testimony, medical proof, signal analysis
  • Argue: "Your body is not their battery."
